OpenAI

Frontier GPT and o-series models behind ChatGPT, the API, and Codex-class coding agents. This page summarizes public product lines and release narratives (March 2026 snapshot); see OpenAI, GPT-4, and ChatGPT for encyclopedic detail.

Architecture & training (overview)

Consumer models are typically decoder-only transformers trained on broad internet, licensed data, and human feedback — with later generations adding multimodal inputs (vision, audio), tool use, and reasoning-time compute for the o-series (chain-of-thought style inference, not one giant frozen CoT in the weights). Exact parameter counts for GPT-4 and GPT-5-class models are often undisclosed; treat tables as directional.

Official hub: openai.com · API docs: Models.

Tiers & naming

  • GPT-3.5 — fast, economical chat class; many SKUs retired over time.
  • GPT-4 — flagship multimodal era (vision in GPT-4 Turbo / 4o paths).
  • GPT-4o (“omni”) — unified multimodal model family (text/image/audio) at lower latency than prior GPT-4 variants in OpenAI messaging.
  • o-series (o1, o3, …) — reasoning-optimized; emphasizes test-time compute and structured problem solving.
  • GPT-5.x — successor generation in product naming (exact versioning follows OpenAI’s marketing and API IDs).

Launch & benchmarks (selected)

GPT-4 (March 2023) set a new bar for general capability vs GPT-3.5; multimodal and larger-context variants followed through 2023–2024. GPT-4o (May 2024) emphasized omnimodal interaction and latency. o1 (2024) introduced explicit reasoning-time scaling for math and code benchmarks.

Competitive framing in industry coverage often references Anthropic Claude, Google Gemini, and xAI Grok in the same cohort by era.
