Arcee AI

Open-weights LLM vendor and model merging stack — MergeKit, Arcee Fusion method, and families such as SuperNova, Caller, and AFM. Strong focus on Arcee Cloud for training/merge/deploy. Less mainstream than OpenAI/Anthropic; important for merge research and enterprise customization (March 2026 snapshot).

← AI Model Museum

Architecture & stack (overview)

Arcee Fusion is a selective merging technique (importance scoring + dynamic thresholds + fusion masks) used to build merged models like SuperNova without blunt full-parameter averaging. MergeKit is the open tooling ecosystem Arcee maintains for community merges. Base architectures often trace to Llama-class and Qwen-class open weights.

Official: arcee.ai · Hugging Face org: arcee-ai

Lines & naming

  • SuperNova — merged high-capability models (e.g. 70B class in press).
  • Medius / Virtuoso — internal merge families used in Arcee Fusion blog posts.
  • Caller — tool-use / API orchestration focused model (Qwen-derived lineage in releases).
  • AFM (Arcee Foundation Model) — compact decoder-only (e.g. 4.5B) with ReLU² activations in research docs.
  • Convo — appears in Arcee’s naming for conversational/merge experiments; verify specific checkpoints on Hugging Face.
